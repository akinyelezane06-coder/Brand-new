About

Porsche originated in 1931 when Ferdinand Porsche founded Dr. Ing. h.c. F. Porsche GmbH in Stuttgart, Germany, as an engineering and design consultancy rather than a car manufacturer. One of the company’s earliest and most influential projects was the development of the Volkswagen Beetle, which helped establish Porsche’s reputation for innovative, efficient engineering. After World War II, Ferdinand’s son, Ferry Porsche, fulfilled his vision of building a sports car under the family name, leading to the creation of the Porsche 356 in 1948. This model marked the beginning of Porsche as an automobile manufacturer and set the foundation for the brand’s identity—combining performance, precision, and timeless design. Over time, Porsche grew into one of the world’s most respected sports car manufacturers, known especially for iconic models like the 911, which embodies the company’s enduring commitment to engineering excellence and driving performance.

Drive

Driving a Porsche is about blending control, awareness, and smooth inputs to fully experience its performance. Start by adjusting your seat and steering wheel so you sit low, upright, and comfortably in control, with easy access to the pedals and clear visibility. Porsche vehicles respond best to smooth acceleration, precise steering, and confident braking, so avoid abrupt movements and let the car’s balance do the work. When accelerating, apply steady throttle to feel the engine’s power build, and when cornering, look ahead and steer smoothly to maintain stability and grip. Overall, driving a Porsche is less about forcing speed and more about precision, rhythm, and enjoying the connection between driver, road, and machine.
